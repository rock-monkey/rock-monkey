TheKFoundation

Also known as TheKLF, The Justified Ancients Of Mu Mu, 2K, The Jams, Disco 2000, One World Orchestra and The Timelords. Side Projects include Space, The Orb, Advanced Acoustic Armaments, and Black Smoke

This article is about the music, the stunts and the events of Bill Drummond and Jimmy Cauty using the moniker TheKFoundation
1. The K-F Prize

The first thing anybody knew about TheKFoundation was when a series of adverts started appearing in national newspapers, declaring such bizarre phrases as "Kick out the clocks" and "K time", followed by "Abandon All Art Now"!

Two weeks later, another advert appeared, announcing "It has come to our attention that you did not abandon all art now. Further direct action is thus necessary. The K Foundation announce the 'mutha of all awards', the 1994 K Foundation award for the worst artist of the year"

And they did. Coincidentally their shortlist was identical to that of the Turner prize, and the same person won. The K-Foundation prize was double that of the Turner prize (£40,000)

The festival took place in a field, and the money was delivered in two bright orange armoured cars, with "K Cera Cera" and ABBA's "Money Money Money" blasting over the radio

(See "Nailed to a wall)

They then returned to the arts gallery, and announced that Rachael Whiteread (the Turner prize winner) had won the prize and if she didn't collect it, it would be burnt. She didn't make an apperance until someone caught sight of Gimpo fiddling with the lighter fluid and matches. The money was donated to needy artists
2. Nailed to a wall

During the K-F Prize, A million pounds in £50 notes was nailed to a large piece of wood and titled "Nailed to a wall". It was then auctioned, with the starting price at half a million, on the grounds that inflation will erode it's value over time, but art is priceless.

Since the money now had holes in it, the Bank of England wouldn't take it back, and fined Bill and Jimmy £9000 for damaging money, and charged them £500 to reprint it.

Sources can't confirm this, but it may have given them ideas for their next stunt...
3. 'Watch the K Foundation Burn a Million Quid'

They did. It was filmed by Gimpo, and took place on the isle of Jura, Scotland. Yes, they really did it. There is video footage, witnesses, the bank confirmed it, and burnt £50 notes kept appearing over the isle for the following few days.

Bill and Jimmy brought back around £80,000 of it (partially burnt) and paid a potter to bake them into a brick. Which he did. And then they filmed it. It's called "The Brick". It's a 3-minute shot of a brick. So there you are.

The film was taken around festivals and the like for a while, but interest was waning. Both for the audience and for the artists. So Bill and Jimmy signed a contract not to talk about the incident for 23 years. (on the bonnet of a car which they then pushed into the sea)
4. K-Cera-Cera

"Available nowhere ... no formats" and not planned for release until world peace was established. At least, that's what the adverts said. But it did get a very limited release (3000) in Israel following the peace accord.

Bill and Jimmy actually got the Red Army Choir to sing a version of "Que Cera Cera", followed by "War Is Over (If You Want It)" (yes, *that* song). The end result is interesting, and certainly enjoyable
