Zork

Zork is a great InteractiveFiction (a.k.a. text-based adventure) game made by Marc Blank and David Lebling and distributed in the late seventies by Infocom, who were - at the time - the world's biggest distributer of InteractiveFiction games. It comes in three parts (Zork I, Zork II, and Zork III), and features several awful puzzles (such as the "diamond floor" room, the carousel, and the loud room), and several mazes in the confusing style that was traditional for adventure games of it's age. It invented the Grue, which would eat the adventurer if they spent too long wandering around in the dark, and this character was later parodied in many other adventure games, including by the modern Zork games. It's not possible to call yourself a serious InteractiveFiction player without ever having played Zork.
It's now free to download: get it here.
Following the success of the original Zork trilogy, the series was extended (in both directions) in later years, with games like ZorkNemesis, BeyondZork, Zork: Grand Inquisitor, ZorkQuest and ZorkQuest II, ZorkZero, Sorcerer, the Enchanter series, Spellbreaker, SpiritWrak, Zork: The Undiscovered Underground, ReturnToZork, Wishbringer, and Balances. Not to mention the stuff contained in the NewZorkTimes. The entire genre has been parodied by games such as Dork.
