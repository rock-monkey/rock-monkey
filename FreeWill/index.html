Also an abbreviation of the popular nineties film Free Willy. Gareth Hopkins
If free will is a 'figment of our imagination' then it is no more or less so than anything else we perceive. 'Figment of the imagination' is derogatory and implies some kind of misguidedness, invalidity or even childishness. I would prefer "construct of the human 'mind'" - a definition which can be applied to anything you see, touch, smell, taste, hear, remember, think, etc etc... Since nothing exists outside our minds we have as much free will as we need, which is as much free will as we can subjectively experience. Even if you believe the theories which state that free will does not truly exist to be correct (which I do) then you cannot ever truly experience this to be the case as you perceive choices to be available to you and make them accordingly. The point at which a prediction could realistically be made, at a human level, would only be after the fact of you having made the choice, which isn't really a prediction. The prediction could only be made by what you might call 'God' - but that's effectively a different debate. The prediction would be made by something which underlay the universe on some level whether that was metaphysical, spiritual, psychological, neuropsychological or smaller. We are not beings which exist at such a level, we cannot perceive beyond our minds and our minds tell us we have free will. The level at which free will can be proven emphatically to not exist is imperceptible and hypothetical - not to mention impractical. It is correct, but correct at a point of irrelevance. At least if you want to deal with people on the level of Being People. Rather than on some hypothetical philosophical plain underpinned by some transcendental grand narrative, which is other than God but no more realistic. - Restless Boy.
