Bonfire Night

A night the Engish celebrate the failure of a revolution. People watch FireWorks together etc.

Interestingly, we celebrate the failure of this famous act of terrorism by lighting fires and by blowing things up. It could be argued - particularly as we've actually since all-but removed the power of the reining monarch anyway - that we're actually celebrating GuyFawkes and his buddies' famous stunt itself. Following this logic, perhaps next year I'll celebrate "9/11 Day" and fly radio controlled planes into buildings. - Dan Huntley
Here in AberWorld, every night can be bonfire night!
