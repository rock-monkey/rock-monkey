Existentialist

Someone who believes life to be somewhat absurd and that humankind is left alone in the universe to create their own individual morality and purpose, this is a blessing in terms of freedom but a curse in terms of the absolute responsibility with which it endows one. The only real moral rules of existentialism itself are 1 not to decieve yourself and 2 not to hide from others the realisation of their own truth, everything else is left to the individual.
The best overall description of existentialism comes from a small and easily accessible (AlecR has the attention span of a flea but still managed to.... yadda yadda) book recording a famous lecture given by JeanPaulSatre defending his philosophy to the Communists called Existentialism est un humanisme loosely translated as existentialism and humanism
Quite how AlecRichardson reconciles this with his christianity is somwhat a mystery, even to him, but that's the beauty of existentialism. Sooren Kikergaard was probably the first real existentialist (although kikergaard was actually a pseudonym for a bizzare trio of philosophical writers) and "he" was a christian, he chose christianity knowing that it was flawed. Satre sees christianity and existentialism as completely incompatible, one of the birthplaces of existentialism is in FriedrichNietzsche's statement "God is Dead".
