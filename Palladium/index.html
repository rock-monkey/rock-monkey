Palladium

Warning :- Biased

Part of a much bigger concern known as Trusted Computing, but I'm putting the main article here because I like the name of the Microsoft Windows segment (now known as NGSCB)

Trusted Computing. Sounds innocent. It's not. It's a conspiracy designed to ensure you never have full control of your computer again.

What is it? Ask an official and they'll probably fob you off with something about how it enables you to experience a whole new wealth of multimedia. Wrong. We already have access to as much as we want. Restricting our access isn't going to make it any easier

Some stages of Trusted Computing have already come into effect. The ultimate aim is to provide an encrypted "service" from all levels. From the file you download right through to the circuit board. And it seems to be working. WindowsXP and Windows 2000 (Service Pack 3 (I think) onwards) come with DRM services built in.

Now, this is all very well. You don't have to use it. You can always make your own, unencrypted digital copies. For now at least. They're trying to discourage that by issuing Protected Music Cds. Should you have to pay again? No, but they'd like you to

What about this you may have heard about self-deleting documents which can now be produced by Microsoft Office? The ability to create no paper-trail is a scary concept, and will probably just encourage to be even more reckless and carefree than they already are. Send a nasty email and have it remove all the evidence for you! As if Microsoft Word documents keeping previous versions wasn't bad enough...

It gets worse. There are plans to issue Operating Systems which are themselves "secure". They will negotiate with the host PC and will not even boot if the environment doesn't match what they were expecting. Sound far fetched? Well... what do you think the WindowsXP activation is for? You change your hardware, you have to re-register. Whoopeee

It gets worse. Again. There are plans to make applications follow the same steps (Indeed, Microsoft Office does). If this continues, it will just about kill shareware, OpenSource software, and indeed OpenSource Operating Systems as we know them. Of course, this can't happen without support from the hardware manufacturers. Shame Intel are already a part of the TCPA isn't it?

It may sound far fetched, and it may sound as though I'm exaggerating here, but we aren't that far off. The TCPA has already formed, ideas have been discussed and some have already been implemented. We've proved time and time again that ideas like CSS, DRM and ProductActivation can be circumvented, but if they do this on a hardware level, we won't even be able to run the tools needed to circumvent them! We are already "advised" by Microsoft that we shouldn't install drivers that haven't been "cerfified" by them (By this, of course, they mean that the manufacturers didn't pay them large sums of money to slap a "Designed for Windows XP" sticker on their product). Some implementations question any programs you try and run which aren't recognised. How long before they just say no?

How long before you do?
