WesternWorld

Techinically speaking the world doesn't have a "west" bit to it- there is no west pole, you just keep on going round and round. The term dates back to when the European nations were expanding ever outwards, and they still thought the world was flat, with them at the western-most edge, and Asia towards the eastern-most edge. With the discovery of the Americas, the European explorers and wise men decided the world was in fact round, but the "East" and "West" were so engrained into people's minds that they stayed there, and we still use them today.
When people say "the Western World " they tend to mean the rich part of it. Although Africa and Central and Southern America are in the west, people most often group them into the "developing countries" camp.
