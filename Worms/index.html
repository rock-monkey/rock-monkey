Worms

Worms are the wiggliest of all Animals. They live in soil and are the favourite animal for Children to torture when bored in Gardens, just after Slugs.
If you chop a Worm in two rather than dying it will become two Worms*, which will then continue to be alive and do wiggly things. This brings about the question of whether Worms have a Soul and if so, what it would be like. If one half of a severed worm was sinful, and the other good would it go half to Hell and half to Heaven? Or it, say, spend a week in one then a week in another? If so Worms may be uniquely placed in the AnimalKingdom to comment on the nature of Spirituality. Unfortunately a)they cannot speak and b)they're probably in no mood to further Human knowledge as they keep getting chopped by Children with spades.
* NB: this does not work with Ducks
Also; it's not true. Assuming no fatal damage occurs, the half with the vital organs (look the the lump in particularly fat worms) has some chance to survive and regrow - but it's not a great chance. The remaining half will die. In actual fact, the odds of the worms survival are limited at best if you cut it in half, so please don't do this. More info on the 'half worm myth' from the BBC.
Plus; it certainly doesn't work if you cut it lengthways.
