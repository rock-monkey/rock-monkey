Rock

Rocks and the Minerals that make them up are the tangible record of geological processes.. The minerals of the Earth are understood in terms of their molecular architecture (the way their atoms are arranged in crystal structures). The kinds of atoms and their chemical bonding determine not only the crystal structures but the chemical and physical properties of the mineral, all of which are used for identification. Rocks are divided into three major groups igneous, metamorphic and sedimentary, on the basis of origin. They are further subdivided within each group according to mineral composition and texture, which provide the data to allow interpretation of their origin.
