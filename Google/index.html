Google

Noun// - Popular SearchEngine at http://www.google.com/
Verb// - To use the Google SearchEngine. This new verb is also coming to mean, "to search the web," in light of Google's popularity. Google are trying to discourage this, as it could lead to their name becoming a GenericizedTrademark, which would render it's copyright unenforcable and give way to, for example, somebody to open a search engine called "Google For It" or "Web Google".
The name "Google" is an accidental misspelling of the word googol, which was coined in 1938 by MiltonSirotta, nephew of U.S. mathematician EdwardKasner, to refer to the number represented by 1 followed by a hundred zeros. Google's use of the term reflects the company's mission to organize the immense amount of information available on the Web.
Google uses an algorithm called PageRank (contrast PigeonRank), and several other secret criteria, to rank web pages that match a given search string. This can be abused by use of vast numbers of cleverly-crafted hyperlinks - a GoogleBomb - which can result in forcing certain results to appear more relevant in Google than they perhaps should be. PageRank results in strange UsageStatistics on wiki-based web sites like RockMonkey.
Google not only indexes and caches HTML files but also 13 other file types, including PDF, Word documents, Excel spreadsheets and plain text files. Except in the case of text files, the cached version is a conversion to HTML, allowing those without the corresponding viewer application to read the file.
