ReferentialIntegrity

In DataBase theory, ReferentialIntegrity is a bit like duct tape. It's kept together by good tabular relationships, and can be broken by people deleting records without regard to the consequences.
For example, if somebody went into the back-end of the database and deleted a page, for example, "ThePopeIsDead", this would cause a problem, because MisterJTA's comment on that page was not deleted - it would be left "in the ether", and would be impossible to correct (even by MisterJTA, who should be able to delete his own comments) without another trip to the back-end of the database.
Just an example.
