Erasure

The best SynthPop band *ever*. Members include Vince Clarke and Andy Bell. All others are just for touring
When Erasure formed, Vince already had something of a reputation amongst SynthPop fans from Depeche Mode. He'd already been in TheAssembly and Yazoo in the meantime. Andy hadn't, however he had been in a college band known as Dinger, producing the never-released-except-at-concerts single "Air of Mystery"
They've been around since 1985, and their last single "Breathe" got to number four, showing they are still popular, and not just with their fans. Their highest scoring hit is a tie between "Stop", which got to number two and just missed the ChristmasNumberOne thanks to CliffRichard with "Mistletoe and Wine". Git. The other was "Sometimes".
Okay, they had a number one with "ABBA-Esque", but I don't count that because it's just cover versions
Vince is widely known as a fanatic for synthesizers, and has tried pretty much everything ever made. His sample-CD is called "Lucky Bastard", in reference to how many he has. He's tried everything, including analogue, digital and soft-synths. Has a fondness for Moogs
Erasure hits include: "A Little Respect", "Chains of Love", "Sometimes", "Take a Chance on Me", "Breathe", "Love to Hate You", "Stop", "Always" and "Solsbury Hill"
However, Erasure recommended listening is: "Home", "Hideaway", "Drama (Krucial Mix)", "Fingers and Thumbs (Cold Summers Day)", "Am I Right?", "Spiralling (Orchestral mix)" and "Rock Me Gently"
Frequently confused with the PetShopBoys for some reason
